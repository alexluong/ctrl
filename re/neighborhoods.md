# Neighborhoods

## Detroit City

### 48224 — Morningside / East English Village
- **Price:** $82-148K | **Rent:** $1,200-1,300 | **Yield:** strong
- Neighborhoods: Morningside, East English Village, Cornerstone Village, Chandler Park
- Grosse Pointe border blocks command premiums + better tenants
- 18.2% Section 8 participation
- East English Village is the stronger sub-market (+11.5% listing price YoY)
- Crime: high (D- safety), but trending down citywide
- Schools: D
- **Investor take:** Section 8 cash flow, GP border spillover. Know your blocks.

### 48221 — Fitzgerald / University District / Palmer Park
- **Price:** $141-185K | **Rent:** $1,050-1,650 | **Yield:** moderate
- University District: Detroit's premier residential neighborhood (large Tudors/Colonials)
- Fitzgerald: active revitalization ($4M Fitzgerald Revitalization Project)
- Palmer Park: beautiful historic apartments
- Median income $55.5K — strong for Detroit
- Crime: lower in sub-neighborhoods (Univ District, Sherwood Forest among safest in city)
- **Investor take:** best Detroit-proper zip. Both cash flow AND appreciation. Good for BRRRR/value-add.

### 48219 — Brightmoor / Grandmont / Rosedale Park
- **Price:** $55-200K (extreme variance) | **Rent:** ~$1,200
- Grandmont-Rosedale: one of Detroit's best clusters. Active community orgs, well-maintained brick homes. $200K median (+43% YoY)
- Brightmoor: extremely challenged. High vacancy/blight, rock-bottom prices ($68K)
- Old Redford: transitional ($55K)
- Crime: moderate for Detroit (B rating)
- **Investor take:** MUST cherry-pick blocks. Grandmont-Rosedale = Tier 1-2. Brightmoor = Tier 4.

### 48227 — Littlefield / Grandmont-Rosedale (south)
- **Price:** $75-85K | **Rent:** $910-1,034 | **Yield:** strong
- Budget cash-flow play. Low entry, strong gross yield
- Grandmont-Rosedale portion is better; Littlefield/Fishkorn more challenged
- Crime: moderate for Detroit (B rating)
- Popular with out-of-state Section 8 investors
- **Investor take:** budget cash flow, know your blocks. PM is critical.

### 48228 — Warrendale / Chadsey-Condon / Springwells
- **Price:** $55-86K | **Rent:** $729-1,103 | **Yield:** strong on paper
- Largest Detroit zip by pop (~53K). Median income very low ($31.5K)
- SW Detroit / Springwells: revitalizing Hispanic corridor, Chadsey-Condon up 70% YoY
- Warrendale (south, near Dearborn): Arab-American community, relatively stable
- Northern parts (Joy-Tireman): distressed
- Crime: high
- **Investor take:** SW Detroit pocket has real upside. Rest is speculative/management-intensive.

### 48234 — Conant Gardens / Pershing
- **Price:** $46-70K | **Rent:** $832-1,153 | **Yield:** strong on paper
- Conant Gardens: historic Black middle-class enclave, historic district designation
- Pershing: decent bungalows
- Highland Park border: distressed
- Prices up 34.6% YoY — market being "discovered"
- Crime: high
- **Investor take:** deep value / speculative. Conant Gardens is the gem. Experienced investors only.

## Metro Detroit Suburbs

### Redford Township
- **Price:** $176-189K | **Rent:** $1,445-1,500 | **Yield:** meets 1% rule
- Class B community. 77.8% homeownership. Median income $69.4K
- 1950s brick bungalows ~1,000 sq ft are the bread and butter
- Extreme rental demand: only 23 rentals available vs 107 for sale
- Crime: moderate (below national avg for robberies/thefts)
- Ford Advanced Manufacturing Center nearby boosts employment
- **Investor take:** best risk-adjusted cash flow in Detroit metro. Hugely popular with investors.

### Warren
- **Price:** $155-211K | **Rent:** $948-1,390 | **Yield:** moderate
- Largest Macomb County city (~137K pop). Very competitive (sells in ~19 days)
- GM Tech Center, defense contractors, manufacturing base
- South Warren: more affordable, higher yield. North Warren: more suburban
- Crime: moderate (40/1K), improving
- Schools: F
- **Investor take:** scale opportunity. Diverse economy, big population, steady demand.

### Eastpointe
- **Price:** $150-160K | **Rent:** ~$1,320 | **Yield:** moderate
- Formerly "East Detroit" — rebranded 1992
- Brick homes 1950s-1980s, well-maintained
- Crime: HIGH (12th percentile nationally) — significant negative
- Grosse Pointe border provides some demand spillover
- **Investor take:** affordable Macomb entry but crime drags. Cash flow over appreciation.

### Roseville
- **Price:** $163-165K | **Rent:** ~$1,073 | **Yield:** tight
- Blue-collar suburb. I-94/I-696 access. Sells fast (~15 days)
- Crime: moderate, dropping 10% YoY
- Schools: C (slightly better than neighbors)
- **Investor take:** steady, improving. Tighter yields but more predictable. Balanced CF + appreciation.
