# RE Notes

## Market Context (Detroit, March 2026)

- Citywide violent crime dropping significantly through 2025-2026
- Detroit proper still has block-by-block variance — zip codes alone don't tell the story
- Metro suburbs (Redford, Warren, Roseville) are where most investors start
- Section 8 is a major strategy in Detroit city zips
- Redford tip from Mike: target under $130-140K, rent $1,500-1,600. Run the numbers.
- DSCR loans at 7%+ rates — need strong rent-to-price ratio to make the math work
- Foreign national lending options exist but limited and higher cost

## Brokerage Fee Note

FRT (Mike's brokerage) has a $4,000 minimum fee. Seller pays 3% commission, but on cheaper properties (e.g. $78K × 3% = $2,340) the buyer side may need to cover the gap to reach the $4K min. Factor this into offers.

## Strategy Observations

- Current portfolio (2 SFHs, no loans) = strong equity base for DSCR or cash-out refi later
- All-cash + refi later (BRRRR) is the playbook for Detroit city deals
- Metro deals ($130-150K) will need DSCR from the start
- MFH (duplex) strategy makes sense for scaling — 2 rent checks, 1 roof
- Value-add projects capped at $100-120K total keep risk manageable
