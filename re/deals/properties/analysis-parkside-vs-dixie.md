## Revised Analysis

**The rate is everything.** At Mike's 6.25%, both properties cashflow modestly. At 7%+, neither cashflows — they're negative every month.

**This is NOT a cashflow play at 7%.** If your actual DSCR rate is 7%+, you're buying for:
1. Appreciation (Parkside ARV $160K = $31K instant equity)
2. Debt paydown (~$100/mo principal reduction)
3. Tax benefits
4. Long-term rent growth

**Parkside still wins** — better location, $31K instant equity, lower taxes, and it bleeds less at higher rates.

**Dixie is tough to justify at 7%+.** Negative cashflow, no instant equity (ARV = purchase price), and higher taxes.

**Critical next step: get Rene's actual rate quote.** If it's 6.25-6.5%, both deals work. If it's 7%+, you need to decide if you're comfortable with negative cashflow in exchange for equity/appreciation.

**Negotiation becomes even more important.** At 7%:
- Parkside at $120K instead of $129K → cashflow goes from -$22 to ~+$40/mo
- Dixie at $125K instead of $135K → cashflow goes from -$77 to ~-$10/mo
