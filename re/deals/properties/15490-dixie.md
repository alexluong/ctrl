## My Analysis

Dixie is the weaker deal compared to Parkside. The numbers work on paper at 6.25% but are fragile — rate sensitivity is the biggest risk. At 7%+ (which is more likely for a foreign national DSCR), this property barely breaks even on monthly cashflow.

The bull case: Redford is appreciating, Mike knows the block, the property is in good shape, and the fell-through deal gives negotiating leverage. If you can get it at $125K, the numbers improve meaningfully.

The bear case: thin cashflow, rate risk, and you're paying $135K for 912sqft in Redford when Parkside offers a better location and better cash flow for $129K.

**If I had to pick one: Parkside over Dixie.** If doing both, Dixie needs a price cut to $125K or below to make the risk/reward work.
